<p><p>The thorium reactor market encompasses various reactor types, each with distinct features. Heavy Water Reactors (PHWRs) utilize heavy water as a moderator, while High-Temperature Gas-Cooled Reactors (HTRs) use graphite moderation and gas cooling. Boiling Water Reactors (BWRs) and Pressurized Water Reactors (PWRs) are light water reactors that manage steam generation differently. Fast Neutron Reactors (FNRs) harness fast neutrons for fission, while Molten Salt Reactors (MSRs) use liquid salt for fuel and cooling. Accelerator Driven Reactors (ADS) combine particle accelerators with reactors for enhanced safety and efficiency, advancing the thorium utilization potential.</p></p>
