<p><p>The wire binding machine market is primarily segmented by binding types, namely 2:1 (21 holes) and 3:1 (34 holes). The 2:1 binding method features 21 holes along the edge of the document and is commonly used for thicker materials, providing a sturdy hold. In contrast, the 3:1 binding method includes 34 holes, allowing for a tighter, more flexible bind suitable for smaller documents. Each type caters to specific user needs, influencing purchasing decisions based on document thickness and presentation requirements.</p></p>
